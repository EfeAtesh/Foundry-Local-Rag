# Vehicle Fixing & Maintenance Guide
*Based on University Extension Automotive Technology and basic roadside emergency manuals.*

## 1. Engine Will Not Start (Troubleshooting)
If your car doesn't start, follow this systematic diagnostic path:
*   **Case A: No sound or clicking when turning the key.**
    *   *Cause:* Dead or weak battery, loose battery terminals, or a failed starter motor.
    *   *Action:* Check battery terminals for corrosion (white crust). Tighten loose terminals. Try jump-starting the vehicle.
*   **Case B: Engine cranks (makes a whirring sound) but won't start.**
    *   *Cause:* Fuel delivery issue (empty tank, failed fuel pump) or ignition issue (bad spark plugs).
    *   *Action:* Verify fuel gauge. Check fuel pump fuse. Listen for a humming sound from the fuel tank when turning the key to the 'ON' position.

## 2. Dealing with Engine Overheating
If your engine temperature gauge rises into the red zone or steam is coming from under the hood:
1.  **Pull over safely:** Immediately turn off the air conditioning and turn on the heater to maximum to pull heat away from the engine.
2.  **Do NOT open the radiator cap immediately:** The coolant system is under extreme pressure. Opening it while hot will cause explosive boiling and severe burns. Wait at least 30-45 minutes for the engine to cool down.
3.  **Inspect coolant level:** Once cool, check the coolant reservoir tank. Top up with a 50/50 mixture of antifreeze and water if low.
4.  **Check for leaks:** Inspect the radiator hoses for visible cracks, splits, or pooling liquid.

## 3. How to Change a Flat Tire
*   **Step 1:** Park on a flat, solid surface. Engage the parking brake and place wheel chocks behind the opposite wheels.
*   **Step 2:** Use the lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts on the flat tire by one turn (do not remove them yet).
*   **Step 3:** Place the jack under the vehicle's designated jack points (consult car manual). Raise the vehicle until the tire is 6 inches off the ground.
*   **Step 4:** Remove the lug nuts fully, pull off the flat tire, and mount the spare tire.
*   **Step 5:** Tighten the lug nuts by hand, lower the vehicle until the tire touches the ground, then tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern using the wrench. Lower the jack fully.
