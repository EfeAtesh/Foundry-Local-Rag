# Wilderness Survival Guide
*Based on the US Army Survival Manual (FM 3-05.70 / FM 21-76) and FEMA emergency guidelines.*

## The SURVIVAL Acronym
To survive in any wilderness environment, remember the core military survival acronym:
*   **S** - Size up the situation (evaluate your surroundings, physical condition, and equipment).
*   **U** - Undue haste makes waste (stay calm, plan your actions, do not panic).
*   **R** - Remember where you are (orient yourself with maps, compass, or natural signs).
*   **V** - Vanquish fear and panic (panic leads to exhaustion and poor decisions).
*   **I** - Improvise (use natural materials to replace missing gear).
*   **V** - Value life (maintain a positive mental attitude and determination).
*   **A** - Act like the locals (observe animal behavior for food, water, and shelter locations).
*   **L** - Learn basic skills (know shelter building, firecraft, and water purification).

## Emergency Shelters
Your primary concern in extreme weather is protecting your body from heat loss or heat stroke.
1.  **Debris Hut:** A simple shelter made by placing a long ridgepole against a tree trunk or A-frame, then layering branches and leaves on both sides. Insulate the floor with 6 inches of dry leaves.
2.  **Lean-To:** A shelter made by securing a horizontal crossbar between two trees and leaning branches against it at a 45-degree angle. Cover the branches with brush, leaves, or a tarp.
3.  **Snow Cave:** Built in deep snowpack by digging a tunnel and expanding a dome-shaped chamber. Ensure a ventilation hole is kept open in the roof.

## Basic Navigation without a Compass
*   **Shadow-Tip Method:** Place a stick vertically in the ground. Mark the tip of its shadow with a pebble (West). Wait 15-20 minutes and mark the new shadow tip (East). Draw a line between them to find the West-East axis.
*   **Polaris (North Star):** In the Northern Hemisphere, locate the Big Dipper constellation. Draw a straight line through the two stars at the outer edge of the dipper's bowl to locate Polaris, which indicates true North.
